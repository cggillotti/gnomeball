Fastball

A steady pitch that will improve at a pace similar to the scrapper and pitcher’s batting ability. Fatty’s will eventually crush it.

Sinker

A variation of the fastball,this pitch becomes extremely hittable at higher levelsbut it triggers agroundball bonus for the fielding save.

Splitter (limited, 1 per inning)

The forkball is a filthy good pitch on opening day, butby the end of theseason most good batters have figured it out and most good pitchers have stopped throwing it.

Knuckleball

This pitch hasthe most variability in quality. It is usually very difficult to hit, but when contact does happen, it triggers the regular power roll as well as a special d2 for the batter; this special roll is separate scoring and cannot be prevented by fielding, even if the fielder reduces the regular powerto zero and records an out.Additionally, possessing a knuckleball stunts the growth of the other pitches, with neither being allowed to improve per level.

Screwball (limited, 1 per inning)

Not much is known about this reverse curveball. Perhaps some brave gnome will spend a career mastering it.

Spitball (limited, ump check)

Must pass umpire inspection( d4 AC 4)after each successful quality roll; if it fails inspection, it is suspended for the game; after twosuspensionsit is lost for the season andthe pitcher must replace it with a Level 1 pitch)—The shineball is illegal for a reason. It killed a gnome in the only on-field death the sport has known. It’s also gross. But holy hell, it’s unhittable.If winning’sall that matters to you, lubeup!

Meatpitch

This is a batting practice fastball at best. When contact does happen, it triggers the regular powerroll as well as aspeciald6for Fatties, d4for Scrappers, andd2 for Hurlers; this specialroll is separate scoringand cannot be prevented by fielding, evenif the fielder reduces the regular powerroll to zeroand records an out.

Eephus

This novelty pitch is a fan favorite, both for its majestically looping arc over the plate, and for its laser beam trajectory over the wall. When contact does happen, it triggers theregularpowerrollas well as aspeciald6for Fatties, d4for Scrappers, and d2 for Hurlers; this special roll is separate scoring and cannot be prevented by fielding, even if the fielder reduces the regular powerroll to zero and records an out.

Curveball (limited, 1 per inning)

The Hook. The Deuce. Uncle Charlie. The ol’Snapdragon! This is the pitch that’s ruining the sport. But is it here to stay?

Changeup (limited, follows fastball/sinker)

The off-speed pitch has a relatively weak initial roll, but as a bonus addshalf the value of the previous pitch’s quality roll.
